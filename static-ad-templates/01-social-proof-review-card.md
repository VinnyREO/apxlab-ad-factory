# Template 01: Social Proof / Review Card

## Description
A UGC-style product shot combined with a customer testimonial quote bubble. Feels native to social feeds — like a friend sharing their experience, not a brand advertising.

## Visual Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│                             │
│   [UGC PRODUCT PHOTO]       │
│   POV/first-person angle    │
│   Hand holding product      │
│   Casual setting (desk,     │
│   kitchen, bathroom)        │
│                             │
├─────────────────────────────┤
│ ┌─────────────────────────┐ │
│ │ 💬 "[TESTIMONIAL]"      │ │
│ │                         │ │
│ │ — [REVIEWER NAME]       │ │
│ │   ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐              │ │
│ └─────────────────────────┘ │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```

### Zones
- **Hero Zone (60-70%):** POV photo of hand holding/using product. Casual, lived-in background (laptop, kitchen counter, bathroom mirror). Natural lighting.
- **Quote Zone (30-40%):** White or light speech-bubble overlay with bold testimonial text. Reviewer name + star rating. Optional verified badge.

## Copy Structure

### Testimonial Formula
> "[Specific visceral result] [timeframe]. [What they tried before]. [Emotional payoff]."
> — [First Name] [Last Initial].

### Placeholders
- `[TESTIMONIAL]` — Specific, visceral customer result (the more specific, the more believable)
- `[REVIEWER_NAME]` — First name + last initial
- `[PRODUCT]` — Product name
- `[BENEFIT_1]` — Primary benefit experienced
- `[TIMEFRAME]` — How long it took

## Copy Variations

### Variation A: Visceral Health Result
**Ad Copy:**
> "[BENEFIT_1] for the first time in [TIMEFRAME]. I've tried everything — [PAIN_POINT] was ruining my life. [PRODUCT] changed everything."
> — [REVIEWER_NAME] ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Body text:** I was skeptical. After years of trying [PAIN_POINT] solutions that never worked, I almost gave up. Then a friend recommended [PRODUCT]. Within [TIMEFRAME], [BENEFIT_1]. I wish I'd found this sooner.

### Variation B: Comparison to Alternatives
**Ad Copy:**
> "I've spent $[STAT] on [PAIN_POINT] products that didn't work. [PRODUCT] actually delivered in [TIMEFRAME]."
> — [REVIEWER_NAME] ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Body text:** After wasting money on [PAIN_POINT] solutions, I was ready to give up. [BRAND] was different from day one. [BENEFIT_1], [BENEFIT_2], and I finally feel like myself again. [CTA]

### Variation C: Emotional Transformation
**Ad Copy:**
> "My [family member] said, '[emotional observation about the change].' That's when I knew [PRODUCT] was working."
> — [REVIEWER_NAME] ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Body text:** [PAIN_POINT] was affecting everything — my confidence, my relationships, my energy. [TIMEFRAME] after starting [PRODUCT], the people around me noticed before I did. [BENEFIT_1]. [CTA]

## Image Generation Prompt

```
A first-person POV photograph of a [skin tone] hand holding a [product type: bottle/jar/packet/gummy] 
of [PRODUCT] in front of a [casual setting: laptop on desk / kitchen counter / bathroom mirror]. 
Natural, warm lighting from a window to the [left/right]. The product label shows [BRAND] branding 
in [brand colors]. Background is slightly blurred (shallow depth of field). The mood is casual and 
authentic, like a real person's photo, not a studio shot. Shot on iPhone. Aspect ratio 4:5.

Below the photo, overlay a white rounded speech-bubble with the testimonial text in bold dark gray, 
with the reviewer name in lighter gray below it and 5 gold star icons.
```

## Real Examples from Research

- **K9 Vitality** — POV hand-holding gummy supplement in front of laptop. Testimonial: "bowel movements are normal for the 1st time in 15 years" — Wendy B. Visceral specificity = believability.
- **Living Root** (Ceylon Cinnamon) — Product shot with review card overlay, "Actually Do The Job" headline.
- **Ageless Beauty Secrets** — "My daughter said, 'Mom, you're smiling...'" — emotional third-party observation.

## Best For
- Supplements (gut health, vitamins, wellness)
- Skincare products
- Pet health products
- Any product with strong customer testimonials
- Products that solve embarrassing or personal problems (specificity wins)

## Design Tips
- Testimonial should be SPECIFIC, not generic ("lost 12 lbs in 3 weeks" > "great product")
- POV angle makes viewer feel like THEY're holding the product
- Casual setting normalizes the product as part of everyday life
- White quote bubble provides contrast and draws the eye
- Keep reviewer name real-sounding (first name + last initial)
