# Template 02: Us vs Them / Category Reframe

## Description
Positions the entire product category as broken, then reframes YOUR product as the evolution. Uses crossed-out competitor attributes vs. positive brand attributes. Dark, editorial, premium aesthetic.

### Zones
- **Headline Zone (10%):** Editorial headline that challenges the category
- **Rejection Zone (20%):** Crossed-out/negated competitor attributes in pill-shaped tags
- **Reframe Zone (15%):** Bold statement redefining the category
- **Product Zone (30%):** Hero product shot, hand-held or on surface
- **Validation Zone (15%):** Positive attribute callouts flanking the product
- **CTA Zone (10%):** Subscription offer or shop button

## Copy Structure

### Headline Formula
> "The Truth About [CATEGORY]" or "Not All [PRODUCT TYPE] Is Created Equal"

### Reframe Formula
> "This Isn't [OLD CATEGORY WORD]. It's [ELEVATED NEW WORD]."

### Placeholders
- `[CATEGORY]` — The product category (Men's Skincare, Testosterone Supplements, Gut Health)
- `[OLD_FRAME]` — What the category calls itself (anti-aging, supplements, diet pills)
- `[NEW_FRAME]` — Your elevated positioning (cellular remodeling, performance nutrition, metabolic optimization)
- `[BAD_1-3]` — Competitor negatives (Water-Based Formulas, Hormone Disruptors, Artificial Fillers)
- `[GOOD_1-3]` — Your positives (Swiss Purity Protocol, Zero Chemicals, Lab-Tested Ingredients)

## Copy Variations

### Variation A: Category Destruction
**Headline:** The Truth About [CATEGORY]
**Body:** Most [PRODUCT TYPE] products are built on [BAD_1], [BAD_2], and [BAD_3]. [BRAND] isn't [OLD_FRAME]. It's [NEW_FRAME]. Made with [GOOD_1], [GOOD_2], and [GOOD_3]. [CTA]

### Variation B: Money Wasted
**Headline:** Not All [PRODUCT TYPE] Is Created Equal
**Body:** You've been paying for [STAT]mg. You're getting [lower number]mg. [BAD_1] means [percentage]% of what you take is wasted. [BRAND] uses [GOOD_1] for [STAT]% absorption. [CTA]

### Variation C: Scientific Authority
**Headline:** What [AUTHORITY] Won't Tell You About [CATEGORY]
**Body:** [STAT]% of [PRODUCT TYPE] products contain [BAD_1]. The [AUTHORITY] community has known for years that [GOOD_1] outperforms traditional [OLD_FRAME]. [BRAND] is the first to bring [NEW_FRAME] to market. [CTA]

## Real Examples from Research

- **Mythos Men** — "The Truth About Men's Skincare" with crossed-out attributes (Water-Based Formulas, Hormone Disruptors) and reframe: "ISN'T ANTI-AGING. IT'S CELLULAR REMODELING." Dark steel background, hand holding product jar. Score: 41.
- **Alpha Brew** — "Your Morning Coffee Is Killing Your Testosterone." Positions coffee alternatives as superior to traditional supplements. Score: 91.
- **Holistic Hercules Supplements** — "Not all Saw Palmetto is created equal" with #1 Urologist Recommended seal. Navy + gold palette. Score: 61.

## Best For
- Men's health/grooming/skincare
- Premium supplements (testosterone, nootropics, performance)
- Products competing in crowded categories
- Scientific/clinical positioning
- High-ticket products that need to justify price

## Design Tips
- Dark backgrounds = premium, masculine, clinical
- Crossed-out attributes make competitors feel outdated WITHOUT naming them
- The reframe statement is the hook — make it feel like a paradigm shift
- Gold/cream on dark navy = pharmaceutical luxury
- Keep good attributes to 3-4 max for scannability
