# Template 06: Pattern Interrupt / Medical Imagery

## Description
Uses shocking or unexpected medical/scientific imagery (CT scans, lab photos, microscope images) to stop the scroll. Zero or minimal text overlay — the image itself is the hook. The curiosity gap drives the click.

## Visual Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────┐
│                             │
│                             │
│    [MEDICAL/SCIENCE IMAGE]  │
│    Full-frame, dramatic     │
│                             │
│         ⭕ [Annotation]     │
│         (circle/arrow       │
│          highlighting       │
│          key area)          │
│                             │
│                             │
│                             │
└─────────────────────────────┘
```

### Zones
- **Full-Frame Image (100%):** Medical or scientific image fills the entire frame
- **Annotation (overlay):** A single bright circle, arrow, or highlight drawing attention to the key area
- **NO text, NO branding, NO CTA in the image** — all persuasion happens in the ad copy text below

## Copy Structure

### Ad Copy Formula (appears below image in Meta)
> [Curiosity-driven headline that explains the image]
> [2-3 lines connecting the image to a pain point]
> [Product as the solution]
> [CTA]

### Placeholders
- `[MEDICAL_IMAGE_TYPE]` — CT scan, lab test, before/after microscopy, ingredient close-up
- `[ANNOTATION_COLOR]` — Bright green, red, or yellow circle/arrow
- `[PAIN_POINT]` — The condition being addressed
- `[PRODUCT]` — Your product as the solution

## Copy Variations

### Variation A: Diagnostic Reveal
**Image:** CT scan or medical imaging with green circle annotation
**Ad Copy:**
This is what [PAIN_POINT] looks like from the inside. 👆

Most people don't realize [PAIN_POINT] starts [ROOT_CAUSE_LOCATION]. By the time you feel symptoms, [scary consequence].

[PRODUCT] targets the root cause with [KEY_INGREDIENT]. [STAT] of users report [BENEFIT_1] within [TIMEFRAME].

[CTA: Learn What's Really Going On →]

### Variation B: Ingredient Under Microscope
**Image:** Microscope/macro image of a key ingredient or biological process
**Ad Copy:**
This is [KEY_INGREDIENT] at [magnification]x magnification. ☝️

It's the reason [STAT] of [AUTHORITY_TYPE] recommend [PRODUCT] for [PAIN_POINT]. Unlike synthetic alternatives, [KEY_INGREDIENT] works by [mechanism of action].

See why [BRAND] chose it as our #1 active ingredient.

[CTA: See the Science →]

### Variation C: Lab Result Comparison
**Image:** Lab chart, blood work results, or test strip comparison
**Ad Copy:**
These are real lab results. Left: before [PRODUCT]. Right: after [TIMEFRAME]. 📊

[STAT] improvement in [BIOMARKER]. No prescription needed. No side effects reported.

[BRAND] is the only [PRODUCT_TYPE] clinically shown to improve [BIOMARKER] in [TIMEFRAME].

[CTA: See Full Results →]

## Image Generation Prompt

```
Option A - CT Scan:
A clinical coronal CT scan of a human [skull/torso/joint] in grayscale medical imaging style. 
A single bright [green/red/yellow] circle annotation highlights a specific region of interest. 
No text, no branding, no overlays. The image should look like an authentic medical diagnostic 
image. High contrast, clinical, stark. Full-frame. Aspect ratio 4:5.

Option B - Microscope:
A macro/microscope photograph of [ingredient/biological structure] at high magnification. 
Dramatic lighting reveals [texture/structure]. A single bright annotation arrow points to 
the key feature. Scientific, dramatic, beautiful. Full-frame. Aspect ratio 4:5.

Option C - Lab Results:
A close-up photograph of [lab results/blood work/test strips] on a clinical white surface. 
Two results side by side showing clear improvement. Subtle annotation highlighting the 
key difference. Clinical, clean, evidence-based. Aspect ratio 4:5.
```

## Real Examples from Research

- **Sarah Patterson** — CT scan of foot/ankle with green circle annotation. "Read This If You Have Foot Drop ☝️." Zero text on image, all copy in ad body. Score: 61. 6 image variations.
- **Dr. Alex Hair Expert** — "All Hair Treatments Will Fail Until You Fix THIS 👉." Medical/scientific imagery to establish authority. Score: 91.

## Best For
- Health conditions (sinus, joint, gut, hair loss)
- Products with clinical/scientific backing
- Targeting sufferers of specific conditions (pre-qualifies audience)
- Advertorial/educational funnels
- Products that benefit from "shock factor" marketing

## Design Tips
- The image MUST be scroll-stopping — if it looks like everything else in the feed, it fails
- ONE annotation only — too many circles/arrows = cluttered
- Bright annotation color (neon green, red) against grayscale = maximum contrast
- NO text on the image — let the image create curiosity, let the ad copy provide the answer
- This format pre-qualifies audience — only people with the condition will engage
- Works best with "Learn More" CTA, not "Shop Now" — this needs education
- A/B test different medical images — CT scans vs. microscope vs. lab results
