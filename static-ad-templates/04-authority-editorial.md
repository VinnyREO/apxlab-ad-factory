# Template 04: Authority / Editorial Article Card

## Description
Disguised as a health article or editorial piece. Features authority figure (doctor, dermatologist, expert) ranking or recommending products. Looks like native content, not advertising.


### Zones
- **Masthead Zone (8%):** Publication-style header (fake or real publication look)
- **Headline Zone (15%):** Bold, article-style headline with authority figure + ranking
- **Hero Zone (45%):** Clinical image, product comparison, or doctor portrait
- **Intro Zone (22%):** First paragraph teaser that creates curiosity gap
- **CTA Zone (10%):** "Learn More" or "Read the Article" button

## Copy Structure

### Headline Formula
> "[Authority Title] Ranks [Number] [Product Type] That [Outcome]"
> "[Authority Title]: '[Quote about product category]'"
> "The Best [Time-Based] [Product Type] to Take for [Outcome]"

### Intro Formula
> "In this article, a [credential] reveals: [curiosity gap bullet points]"

### Placeholders
- `[AUTHORITY]` — Doctor, Dermatologist, Endocrinologist, Nutritionist
- `[CREDENTIAL]` — Board-certified, Harvard-trained, etc.
- `[NUMBER]` — Products ranked (3-5)
- `[PRODUCT_TYPE]` — Supplement category
- `[OUTCOME]` — Desired result
- `[CURIOSITY_1-3]` — Teaser bullet points

## Copy Variations

### Variation A: Doctor Ranking
**Headline:** Doctor Ranks [NUMBER] [PRODUCT_TYPE] That Help [OUTCOME]
**Body:** In this article, a [CREDENTIAL] [AUTHORITY] reveals:

🔥 The surprising link between [PAIN_POINT] and [related issue]
💊 Why most [PRODUCT_TYPE] don't work (and what to look for instead)
✅ The #1 recommended [PRODUCT_TYPE] based on clinical data

[CTA: Read the Full Article →]

### Variation B: Expert Quote
**Headline:** [AUTHORITY]: "My patients have tested the TOP [NUMBER] [PRODUCT_TYPE]. This one [OUTCOME]."
**Body:** After years of recommending [PRODUCT_TYPE] to patients struggling with [PAIN_POINT], [AUTHORITY] found that most products fall short. Only one consistently delivered [BENEFIT_1] and [BENEFIT_2]. [CTA]

### Variation C: Breaking Research
**Headline:** New Study: The Best [TIME] [PRODUCT_TYPE] to Take for [OUTCOME]
**Body:** [CREDENTIAL] [AUTHORITY] reviewed the latest research on [PRODUCT_TYPE] and identified what actually works vs. marketing hype. The results surprised even the researchers. [CTA: See the Results →]

## Image Generation Prompt

```
A native-content style editorial ad card. At the top, a thin masthead bar with a 
publication-style logo reading "[BRAND] Health" or "Medical Review" in a clean serif font 
on a white background.

Below, a bold headline in dark text: "[Authority] Ranks [Number] [Products] That Help [Outcome]".

The hero image shows either:
Option A: A professional headshot of a [male/female] doctor in a white lab coat with a 
stethoscope, looking directly at camera, against a clean clinical background.
Option B: A flat-lay comparison of [3-4] supplement bottles arranged in a ranking format 
(#1, #2, #3) on a white surface.

Below the image, intro body text in gray with emoji bullet points creating a curiosity gap.
At the bottom, a blue "Learn More" button.

The overall feel should look like a health news article, NOT an ad. 
Clean, editorial, trustworthy. Aspect ratio 4:5.
```

## Real Examples from Research

- **Health Insider** — "The Best Nighttime Metabolism Supplement to Take for Weight [Loss]." Score: 91. Used "U.S. Endocrinologist" authority positioning.
- **Health Insider** — "Doctor Ranks 4 Gut-Healing Supplements That Help Burn Fat." Score: 91. Curiosity bullets with emojis.
- **Dr. Alex Hair Expert** — "All Hair Treatments Will Fail Until You Fix THIS 👉." Score: 91. Authority + curiosity gap.

## Best For
- Health supplements (weight loss, gut health, hormonal)
- Products with clinical studies or research backing
- Higher-ticket products that need education before purchase
- Advertorial funnels (ad → article → product page)
- Products targeting 35+ demographic who trust authority figures

## Design Tips
- CTA should ALWAYS be "Learn More" (not "Shop Now") — this is content marketing
- The article format pre-qualifies interested buyers through education
- Authority titles boost credibility — use real credentials if possible
- Curiosity gap bullets (🔥💊✅) drive click-through
- Keep the design CLEAN — cluttered editorial cards feel like clickbait
- This format works best with advertorial landing pages, not direct product pages
