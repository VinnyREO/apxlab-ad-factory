# Ad Frameworks — UGC Video Ads for DTC Supplements

## The Core DR Formula
**Hook → Problem → Solution → Value Props → Social Proof → CTA**

Can rearrange: problem, USP, or social proof can serve as the hook if attention-grabbing enough.

---

## Framework 1: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
1. **Problem** (0-3s): Pain point the viewer recognizes immediately
2. **Agitate** (3-15s): Intensify — wasted money, failed solutions, frustration
3. **Solve** (15-30s): Product as the natural, logical solution

**Example:**
- Problem: "I was exhausted every afternoon no matter how much I slept"
- Agitate: "I tried every energy drink, more coffee, B12 shots — nothing worked"
- Solve: "Then my nutritionist recommended [Brand] — adaptogenic mushrooms for sustained energy without the crash"

## Framework 2: Testimonial / Social Proof
UGC testimonials dominate 32.6% of top supplement ads.
1. Hook with personal claim or transformation
2. Share the journey (before → discovery → after)
3. Specific results or benefits experienced
4. Recommendation + CTA

## Framework 3: Unboxing + First Impressions
1. Anticipation hook ("Finally got my hands on this...")
2. Package reveal — unboxing moment
3. First use / first taste reaction
4. Genuine emotional reaction
5. Initial impressions and CTA

**Pro tip:** Gifting + unboxing works exceptionally well. Film the gifter's perspective, then cut to recipient's genuine reaction.

## Framework 4: Before/After Transformation
1. Show "before" state (low energy, skin issues, poor sleep)
2. Product as the turning point
3. "After" state with specific, measurable results
4. Time-stamp the journey ("after 30 days...")
5. CTA

**Compliance:** Avoid dramatic before/after photos implying guaranteed results. Focus on experience over visual transformation claims.

## Framework 5: Day-in-My-Life / Routine Integration
1. "Morning routine" or "What I eat in a day" hook
2. Natural product integration
3. Brief benefit callout while using it
4. Continue routine — product feels seamless
5. Soft CTA

## Framework 6: Comparison / Us vs. Them
1. Set up comparison (common solution vs. product)
2. Walk through failing alternatives
3. Product as clear winner

**Pro tip:** Adding humor increases performance. Frame competition as absurd, not just inferior.

## Framework 7: "Stitch Incoming" Objection Buster
Person 1 voices common objection → Person 2 addresses it with product knowledge.

**Example:**
- Person 1: "Why do all sleep aids make me groggy in the morning?"
- Person 2: "Most don't have [specific ingredient] which promotes natural REM sleep. You know who does? [Brand]..."

## Framework 8: GF/BF Angle
Product story from opposite sex perspective: "My girlfriend won't stop talking about this supplement." Taps into the core desire of being well-received by others.

## Framework 9: Rant Format
Banner: "[Topic] RANT" — topic does creative targeting, "rant" preps for hot take. Then pivot into problem → solution with benefits.

## The Complete DR Video Formula

```
[0-3s: EMOTIONAL HOOK]
Pattern interrupt + curiosity gap
Address specific pain point or desire

[3-10s: PROBLEM AMPLIFICATION]
Make them feel the pain of status quo
Name failed alternatives (specificity)
Build empathy / "I've been there"

[10-20s: SOLUTION + MECHANISM]
Introduce product naturally
Explain WHY it works (ingredient/science)
Differentiate from alternatives (USP)

[20-30s: PROOF STACK]
Personal results (specific + timeframed)
Social proof (reviews, press, awards)
Visual evidence

[30-35s: RISK REVERSAL + CTA]
Remove buying objections (guarantee, offer)
Create urgency (limited time, scarcity)
Single, clear call-to-action
End with product in frame
```

## Modular Approach (Key to Scale)
Design ads as modular components:
- 3 hooks × 3 problems × 3 solutions × 2 CTAs = **54 unique ads from 11 clips**
- Mix and match to prevent ad fatigue
- Test hooks independently from body content
